• Bartek Podkowa

    Bartek Podkowa spent years studying to be a scientist at the University of Cambridge, King’s College London and University College London. But one day he found himself looking for a movie he wanted to watch… but which didn’t exist. Very soon he discovered how important filmmaking is to him, and how much impact it can have on a person’s life.

    Now he spends most of his time writing, filming, editing and building Seven Hills into a positive voice in the film industry. And he’s still trying to make the movie that started it all.

  • Chris Rowe

    Chris has 12 years of professional experience as an award winning producer/director, writer, DP and editor, with clients as diverse as Arcade Fire, Glenmorangie or the British Government. In recent years he has pioneered 360 video and virtual reality content creation via VRscape.tv. He is a firm believer in this new art form and virtual reality’s ability to provoke empathy in a much more genuine and direct way than traditional video.

    Prior to filmmaking, he studied Archaeology at UCL and has retained a great passion for all areas of history, with a particular specialisation in the Middle East. Having been born in Paris, he is also a French native speaker.

  • Mesbah Uddin

    As he headed to uni, Mesbah really wanted to be like Josh Lyman but within his first two years at Georgetown University, he realized life was more House of Cards than The West Wing in the nation's capital. After trying various nutty snacks in the wilderness of career indecision, he eventually took a bite out of the forbidden fruit of filmmaking. It was a delicious bite but it was also one that banished him to a life of "artistry"; a dirty, dirty word in the eyes of his family and the Bangladeshi community of Queens, NY. But in the words of Dude Lebowski, "Well, that's just like your opinion man..."

  • Shaun Waldie

    Shaun is a London based photographer and cinematographer, doing work across the board - from events photography to feature films. He started photography when he was 12 due to being underwhelmed with his Mum's attempts on holiday. He has never been without a camera ever since. Since then, he has progressed to cinematography, striving to not only tell stories, but make them look good as well.

  • Simon Norman

    Simon is a professional location sound recordist & production mixer, sound designer, dialogue editor & music for media producer, with many feature film, television, commercial, corporate media & short film production credits to his name. He also believes in dinosaurs.
